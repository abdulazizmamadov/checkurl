A malicious URL is a link that leads to a site that poses a risk to the user or their device. Such sites are often used in phishing attacks to steal personal data or infect devices with malware.

Frequently asked questions

  • How to spot a suspicious URL?

    It can be difficult to tell if a URL is dangerous just by looking at it, especially if it has been shortened. However, if the domain is known, look for typos or altered letters in the link. For example, if you see arnazon.com, someone is clearly trying to trick you with a fake Amazon site. To be on the safe side, use a free URL checking tool like Link Checker or a paid service that includes this feature, like NordVPN.

  • What happens when you click on a malicious link?

    Chances are, nothing will happen. You'll immediately know the site is suspicious and just leave it. However, clicking on such a link can lead to information theft (phishing campaigns are used to steal credit card details, logins, and other personal data) or installation of malware. As a result of downloading, your device will have malware that encrypts data, bombards you with ads, or steals passwords.

  • Can Link Checker check the entire site at once?

    No, Link Checker will only scan the specific URL you entered, not the entire domain.
